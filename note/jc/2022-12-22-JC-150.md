Two papers about the impact ionization coefficients of 4H-SiC are introduced in JC150.

## Impact ionization coefficients of 4H-silicon carbide
    Journal: Appl. Phys. Lett. 85, 1380 (2004)
    Link: https://aip.scitation.org/doi/10.1063/1.1784520
    Author: T.Hatakeyama, et al.

In this paper, the authors present the impact ionization coefficients of 4H-SiC for $<0001>$ and $<11\bar20>$ directions that reproduce avalanche breakdown behavior of $p^+n$ diodes on $<0001>$ and $<11\bar20>$ epitaxial 4H-SiC wafers (see Fig.1). They also discuss the origin of anisotropy of the impact ionization coefficient of 4H-SiC, based on the microscopic description of the impact ionization and the transport physics under high electric field. The electric-field dependence and anisotropy of the impact ionization coefficients of 4H-SiC are obtained. As shown in Fig.2, the obtained impact ionization coefficients show large anisotropy. The fitted parameters of the electron and hole impact ionization coefficients of 4H-SiC are shown in Table.1

From the measurements, the authors extracted effective optical phonon energy, which describes the temperature dependence of the impact ionization coefficients. The analytical model - Hatakeyama model of the anisotropic impact ionization coefficients, which interpolates impact ionization coefficients parallel to a-axis and c-axis, was derived considering the energy balance equation in trasport physics.

The ionization coefficients $\alpha$ can be expressed as:

$$
\alpha = \gamma a e^{-\frac{\gamma b}{E}}
$$

where $E$ is electric field. Different values for $a$ and $b$ are used for elctron and hole, also for different lattice crystal direction. The Table~\ref{tab2} lists the parameters of Hatakeyama mode used in this work. The factor $\gamma$  with the optical phonon energy $\hbar \omega_{op}=0.19~eV$ is a function of temperature dependence parameter:

$$
\gamma = \frac{tanh (\frac{\hbar \omega_{op}}{2kT_{0}})}{tanh (\frac{\hbar \omega_{op}}{2kT})}
$$

where $T$ is temperature ($T_0$=300~K); $k =$ is Boltzmann constant. Consider the direction anisotropy of 4H-SiC wafer with the off-angle $\theta$, the $a_{aniso}$ and $b_{aniso}$ can be expressed as:

$$
a_{aniso}=a_{11 \overline{2} 0}^{\left(\frac{B E_{11 \overline{2} 0}}{b_{11 \overline{2} 0} E}\right)^2} a_{0001}^{\left(\frac{B E_{0001}}{b_{0001} E}\right)^2}
$$

$$
b_{aniso}=B \sqrt{1-A^2\left(\frac{B E_{11 \overline{2} 0} E_{0001}}{E b_{11 \overline{2} 0} E_{0001}}\right)^2}
$$

with $E_{0001}=Ecos\theta$, $E_{11 \overline{2} 0}=Esin\theta$, $A=\log \frac{a_{0001}}{a_{11 \overline{2} 0}}$, $B=\frac{F}{\sqrt{\left(\frac{F_{11 \overline{2} 0}}{b_{11 \overline{2} 0}}\right)^2+\left(\frac{F_{0001}}{b_{0001}}\right)^2}}$. Finally, impact ionization with anisotropy used in our simulation is

$$
\alpha = \gamma a_{aniso} e^{-\frac{\gamma b_{aniso}}{E}}
$$

The fitted parameters for ionization coefficients of the Hatakeyama model are listed as below:
